SUNRISE AND SUNSET

"Sunrise" and "Sunset" refer to the specific times when the sun crosses the horizon, marking the beginning and end of daylight, respectively. These times are crucial components of weather information and can be significant for various activities, including outdoor planning, photography, and understanding daily temperature fluctuations. Here's a breakdown of these terms:

  1. Sunrise:
    • The moment when the upper limb of the sun just starts to appear above the horizon.
    • Marks the beginning of the day and the transition from night to day.
    • Typically expressed in local time and can vary based on geographical location and time of year.
    • Information about sunrise is useful for planning early morning activities and understanding the duration of daylight.

  2. Sunset:
    • The moment when the upper limb of the sun disappears below the horizon.
    • Marks the end of the day and the transition from day to night.
    • Like sunrise, sunset times are expressed in local time and can vary by location and season.
    • Knowledge of sunset times is important for planning evening activities and assessing available daylight.
